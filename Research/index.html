Research Area

Wireless Communications - Signal Processing - MIMO - Physical Layer Security

● Research Focus


Our research at the MBNRG group is dedicated to advancing wireless communications, primarily focusing on 5G and 6G mobile cellular networks. We specialize in the application of physical layer signal processing techniques, exploring various dimensions such as massive MIMO, intelligent surfaces, jamming-resistant communications, machine learning, and cooperative networks. Our interdisciplinary approach aims to tackle challenges in signal processing, network design, and communication security, with the goal of enhancing the performance, reliability, and security of next-generation wireless systems. Through our innovative strategies, we strive to optimize future wireless communication systems.

-Beamforming and 3D Beamforming in 5G/6G Wireless Networks


Beamforming is essential for directing signals towards specific users, enhancing system performance. Our research covers traditional beamforming techniques and 3D beamforming in both conventional and reconfigurable intelligent surface (RIS)-aided networks, focusing on applications in millimeter-wave (mmWave) and massive MIMO systems.
● Key papers:
- Three-Dimensional Beamforming: A new enabling technology for 5G wireless networks
- 3D beamforming in Intelligent Reflecting Surface (IRS)-assisted multi-user cognitive radio networks
- Integrated Access and Backhaul (IAB) in Low Altitude Platforms
- 3D Beamforming in Reconfigurable Intelligent Surfaces-assisted Wireless Communication Networks
- Effect of Users Height Distribution on the Coverage of mmWave Cellular Networks With 3D Beamforming
- Energy efficiency maximization in mmWave wireless networks with 3D beamforming
- Vertical Beamforming in Intelligent Reflecting Surface-Aided Cognitive Radio Networks
- Joint tilt angle adaptation and beamforming in multicell multiuser cellular networks
- Impact of user height on the coverage of 3D beamforming-enabled massive MIMO systems

-Massive MIMO and Cell-Free Massive MIMO


Massive Multiple-Input Multiple-Output (MIMO) systems are crucial for enhancing spectral efficiency and network capacity in 5G/6G networks. Our research explores both centralized massive MIMO systems and decentralized, cell-free massive MIMO architectures, which mitigate interference and improve connectivity in dense user environments.
● Key papers:
- Integrated Access and Backhaul (IAB) in Cell-Free Massive MIMO Systems
- Impact of user height on the coverage of 3D beamforming-enabled massive MIMO systems
- Power Allocation for Downlink Training in Cell-Free Massive MIMO Networks
- QoE-Aware Beamforming Design for Massive MIMO Heterogeneous Networks
- Energy efficiency maximization in FDD massive MIMO systems with channel aging
- Energy Efficient AP Selection for Cell-Free Massive MIMO Systems: Deep Reinforcement Learning Approach

-Machine Learning and AI in Wireless Networks


We leverage machine learning and artificial intelligence to optimize wireless communication networks. Techniques such as deep reinforcement learning and supervised learning are applied to address challenges in resource allocation, interference management, and network security.
● Key papers:
- Energy Efficient AP Selection for Cell-Free Massive MIMO Systems: Deep Reinforcement Learning Approach
- Deep Reinforcement Learning Based Adaptive Modulation With Outdated CSI
- Deep Reinforcement Learning-based Anti-jamming Power Allocation in a Two-cell NOMA Network

-Reconfigurable Intelligent Surfaces (RIS) - Intelligent Reflecting Surfaces (IRS)


RIS technology enables smart wireless environments by dynamically controlling signal reflections. Our research explores the integration of RIS in cognitive radio networks and mmWave communication, focusing on beamforming and energy efficiency.
● Key papers:
- 3D Beamforming in Reconfigurable Intelligent Surfaces-assisted Wireless Communication Networks
- Vertical Beamforming in Reconfigurable Intelligent Surface-aided Cognitive Radio Networks
- AN-aided beamforming for IRS-assisted SWIPT systems
- Secrecy rate maximization in multi-IRS mmWave networks
- Secure Simultaneous Information and Power Transfer for Downlink Multi-User Massive MIMO

Physical Layer Security in Wireless Communications


Ensuring physical layer security is critical as 5G and 6G networks become integral to critical infrastructures. Our work focuses on developing robust security mechanisms to protect against eavesdropping, jamming, and other forms of interference. We explore traditional encryption techniques and advanced methods, such as jamming-resistant communications.
● Key papers:

- Phase Jamming Attack: A Practical Attack on Physical layer-Based Key Derivation
- Jamming-Resistant Receivers for the Massive MIMO Uplink
- Jamming Suppression in Massive MIMO Systems
- Jamming Detection in Massive MIMO Systems
- Direction-based jamming detection and suppression in mmWave massive MIMO networks
- Phase Jamming Attack: A Practical Attack on Physical layer-Based Key Derivation
- Jamming-Robust Uplink Transmission for Spatially Correlated Massive MIMO Systems
- A Comparison of TDD and FDD Massive MIMO Systems Against Smart Jamming
- Energy Efficiency of Massive MIMO Cognitive Radio Networks in Presence of Smart Jamming
- Security Vulnerability of FDD Massive MIMO Systems in Downlink Training Phase
- Attacking Massive MIMO Cognitive Radio Networks by Optimized Jamming
