Research Area

Wireless Communications - Signal Processing - MIMO - Physical Layer Security

● Research Focus


Our research at the MBNRG group is dedicated to advancing wireless communications, primarily focusing on 5G and 6G mobile cellular networks. We specialize in the application of physical layer signal processing techniques, exploring various dimensions such as massive MIMO, intelligent surfaces, jamming-resistant communications, machine learning, and cooperative networks. Our interdisciplinary approach aims to tackle challenges in signal processing, network design, and communication security, with the goal of enhancing the performance, reliability, and security of next-generation wireless systems. Through our innovative strategies, we strive to optimize future wireless communication systems.

-Beamforming and 3D Beamforming in 5G/6G Wireless Networks


Beamforming is essential for directing signals towards specific users, enhancing system performance. Our research covers traditional beamforming techniques and 3D beamforming in both conventional and reconfigurable intelligent surface (RIS)-aided networks, focusing on applications in millimeter-wave (mmWave) and massive MIMO systems.
